# Customer Support Escalation Policy

## Purpose

This policy defines when and how the support team escalates customer issues to engineering.

The goal is to ensure that engineering receives actionable, complete, and prioritized information.

Support should escalate issues when customer impact cannot be resolved through standard troubleshooting or documented operational procedures.

## Pre-Escalation Checklist

Before escalating an issue to engineering, support must verify the customer account, confirm the affected workspace, reproduce or validate the reported behavior, and collect relevant request identifiers.

Support should check the status page, recent incident updates, and known issue tracker before opening a new escalation.

Support must include screenshots, logs, timestamps, request IDs, customer impact description, and steps already attempted.

Escalations without reproduction details or request identifiers may be returned for more information unless the issue appears business-critical.

## Escalation Severity

Critical escalations involve data loss, security risk, production outage, or blocked enterprise customer operations.

High-priority escalations involve major feature degradation, repeated failures, or high-value customer impact.

Normal escalations involve product questions, isolated errors, or non-urgent defects.

## Engineering Handoff

The escalation should identify the customer, workspace, feature area, impact, urgency, and evidence collected.

Engineering should acknowledge critical escalations within fifteen minutes during business hours.

Engineering should acknowledge high-priority escalations within one business hour.

Normal escalations are reviewed during the next triage cycle.

## Ownership

Support owns customer communication until engineering explicitly takes over.

Engineering owns technical investigation after accepting the escalation.

Product may be consulted when expected behavior is unclear.

## Closure

An escalation can be closed when the issue is resolved, a workaround is accepted, or engineering determines that the behavior is expected.

Support should document the resolution in the customer record.
